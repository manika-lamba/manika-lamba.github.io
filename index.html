Manika Lamba

Assistant Professor, University of Oklahoma

Knowledge Infrastructure Computational Social Science Metascience Critical Data Practices

My research investigates how information systems shape what knowledge can be found, interpreted, trusted, and reused. I examine where these systems fall short for particular forms of knowledge and for the communities that rely on them, and I use text mining, large language models, machine learning, and participatory approaches to study these challenges empirically. Through this work, I develop open tools, datasets, and research infrastructures that support more discoverable, accountable, and inclusive information systems.

Author of Text Mining for Information Professionals: An Uncharted Territory
