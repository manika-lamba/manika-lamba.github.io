Manika Lamba

Assistant Professor, University of Oklahoma

Knowledge Infrastructure Computational Social Science Metascience Critical Data Practices

Information systems fail quietly. Metadata errors erase scholarship. Computational tools locked behind programming expertise exclude entire professional communities. Datasets about marginalized groups circulate unchecked, encoding errors the people they represent never consented to and cannot correct.

My research targets three connected failures:

  1. How scholarly knowledge gets organized — and whose knowledge gets systematically harder to find as a result
  2. Why computational methods remain inaccessible to most information professionals, and what infrastructure would close that gap
  3. Where information systems harm the communities they claim to serve

I study these problems empirically using text mining, large language models, and machine learning. The outputs are open tools, audited datasets, and research infrastructures built for discoverability, accountability, and inclusion.

Author of Text Mining for Information Professionals: An Uncharted Territory
